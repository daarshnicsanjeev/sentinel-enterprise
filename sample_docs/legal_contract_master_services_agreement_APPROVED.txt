MASTER SERVICES AGREEMENT

This Master Services Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of January 15, 2026, between
APEX TECHNOLOGY SOLUTIONS, INC., a Delaware corporation ("Service Provider"), and
GLOBALSTREAM MEDIA GROUP LLC, a New York limited liability company ("Client").

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 1 — FORCE MAJEURE CLAUSE
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Neither party shall be liable for any failure or delay in performance under this Agreement
to the extent such failure or delay is caused by circumstances beyond the reasonable control
of the affected party, including but not limited to: acts of God, natural disasters, floods,
earthquakes, fire, epidemic or pandemic, government actions, war, civil unrest, terrorism,
labour strikes not involving the affected party's employees, widespread internet or
telecommunications outages, or interruption of essential utility services.

Force Majeure Notice Requirement: The affected party must notify the other party in writing
within five (5) business days of the onset of the force majeure event, specifying the nature,
anticipated duration, and steps being taken to mitigate the impact. Failure to provide timely
notice shall not excuse performance unless notice was itself made impossible by the same event.

Duration and Termination Right: If a force majeure event persists for more than sixty (60)
consecutive calendar days, either party may terminate the affected Statement of Work without
penalty by providing fifteen (15) days' written notice. Pre-paid fees for undelivered Services
shall be refunded on a pro-rata basis within thirty (30) days of termination.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 2 — LIMITATION OF LIABILITY
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

2.1  Aggregate Cap: EXCEPT FOR (I) WILFUL MISCONDUCT OR FRAUD, (II) INDEMNIFICATION
OBLIGATIONS UNDER SECTION 8, AND (III) BREACH OF CONFIDENTIALITY UNDER SECTION 6,
EACH PARTY'S TOTAL CUMULATIVE LIABILITY TO THE OTHER PARTY ARISING OUT OF OR RELATING
TO THIS AGREEMENT OR ANY STATEMENT OF WORK SHALL NOT EXCEED THE TOTAL FEES PAID OR
PAYABLE UNDER THE RELEVANT STATEMENT OF WORK IN THE TWELVE (12) MONTHS PRECEDING THE
CLAIM.

2.2  Exclusion of Consequential Damages: IN NO EVENT SHALL EITHER PARTY BE LIABLE FOR
ANY INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, PUNITIVE, OR CONSEQUENTIAL DAMAGES, INCLUDING LOSS
OF REVENUE, LOSS OF PROFITS, LOSS OF BUSINESS, LOSS OF DATA, LOSS OF GOODWILL, OR
COST OF SUBSTITUTE SERVICES, EVEN IF ADVISED OF THE POSSIBILITY OF SUCH DAMAGES.

2.3  Exceptions to Limitation: The limitation of liability in Section 2.1 shall not apply to:
(a) claims for death or personal injury caused by negligence; (b) claims arising under
applicable consumer protection statutes that cannot be contractually limited; or
(c) amounts awarded by a court for wilful infringement of intellectual property rights.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 3 — DISPUTE RESOLUTION CLAUSE
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

3.1  Escalation: Either party may escalate a dispute to senior management by written notice.
The parties' designated senior representatives shall meet (in person or virtually) within ten
(10) business days of such notice to attempt resolution in good faith.

3.2  Mediation: If the dispute remains unresolved after thirty (30) days of escalation,
either party may refer the matter to non-binding mediation administered by JAMS under its
Streamlined Mediation Rules. The cost of the mediator shall be borne equally.

3.3  Arbitration: Any unresolved dispute shall be submitted to final and binding arbitration
administered by the American Arbitration Association ("AAA") under its Commercial Arbitration
Rules. The arbitration shall be conducted by a single arbitrator agreed by the parties, or
appointed by AAA in default of agreement. The seat of arbitration shall be New York, New York.
The language of arbitration proceedings shall be English.

3.4  Governing Law: This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with
the laws of the State of New York, excluding its conflict-of-law principles.

3.5  Confidentiality of Proceedings: All arbitration proceedings, awards, and settlements
shall be treated as confidential information by both parties.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 4 — SERVICES AND DELIVERABLES
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Service Provider shall perform technology consulting, software development, and managed IT
services as described in individual Statements of Work executed from time to time. Each
Statement of Work shall specify: scope of services, deliverables, timelines, acceptance
criteria, and applicable fees.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 5 — FEES AND PAYMENT
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Fees shall be invoiced monthly in arrears. Payment is due within thirty (30) days of invoice
date. Late payments shall accrue interest at 1.5% per month. The annual master retainer for
this Agreement is USD 1,200,000, exclusive of expenses and applicable taxes.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 6 — CONFIDENTIALITY
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Each party agrees to maintain in strict confidence all Confidential Information received from
the other party, using the same degree of care it applies to its own confidential information
(no less than reasonable care), for a period of five (5) years from disclosure.
