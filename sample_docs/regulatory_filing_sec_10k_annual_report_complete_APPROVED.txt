MATERIAL DISCLOSURES

ITEM 1A. RISK FACTOR DISCLOSURES
Investing in our common stock involves a high degree of risk. You should carefully consider the following risk factors, as well as the other information contained in this Annual Report, before making an investment decision.

REGULATORY AND CLINICAL RISKS:
Our product candidates are subject to extensive regulation by the FDA and comparable foreign regulatory bodies. We cannot guarantee that any of our product candidates will receive regulatory approval. If we fail to obtain regulatory approval for our product candidates, we will be unable to commercialize them, and our business will be materially adversely affected.

Our clinical trials may fail to demonstrate the safety and efficacy of our product candidates to the satisfaction of applicable regulatory authorities. Positive results in preclinical studies or earlier clinical trials do not necessarily mean that later clinical trials will succeed. If our clinical trials fail or produce inconclusive results, we may be required to conduct additional clinical trials, which would result in significant delays and increased costs.

COMPETITION AND MARKET RISKS:
The biopharmaceutical industry is highly competitive. We face competition from major pharmaceutical companies including Pfizer, Merck, AstraZeneca, and numerous biotechnology companies. Many of our competitors have substantially greater financial resources, larger research and development staffs, and more extensive manufacturing and marketing capabilities than we do.

FINANCIAL RISKS:
We have incurred significant losses since our inception and expect to incur losses for the foreseeable future. As of December 31, 2025, we had an accumulated deficit of $478.3 million. We will need substantial additional funding to continue our operations, and such funding may not be available on acceptable terms, or at all.

Our internal control over financial reporting is a process designed to provide reasonable assurance regarding the reliability of financial reporting and the preparation of financial statements for external purposes in accordance with generally accepted accounting principles.

ITEM 7. MANAGEMENT'S DISCUSSION AND ANALYSIS OF FINANCIAL CONDITION

OVERVIEW
NexaGen Biomedical Corporation is a clinical-stage biopharmaceutical company focused on discovering, developing, and commercializing novel therapeutics for autoimmune and inflammatory diseases. Our lead product candidate, NXG-401, is currently in Phase 3 clinical trials for moderate-to-severe rheumatoid arthritis.

LIQUIDITY AND CAPITAL RESOURCES
As of December 31, 2025, we had cash, cash equivalents, and short-term investments of $424.1 million. We believe this is sufficient to fund operations into the first quarter of 2028. We have never generated positive cash flow from operations.
