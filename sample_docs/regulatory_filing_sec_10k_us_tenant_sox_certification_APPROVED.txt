PART I — MATERIAL DISCLOSURE STATEMENT
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

This Annual Report on Form 10-K contains the material disclosure statement required by
the Securities Exchange Act of 1934. Pinnacle Aerospace Technologies Corp. ("Company")
is a leading manufacturer of advanced propulsion systems, autonomous navigation software,
and satellite components for commercial and government aerospace customers.

Material Disclosures for Fiscal Year 2025:
- Revenue: USD 3.42 billion (+18% YoY); Operating Income: USD 612 million
- Awarded a USD 890 million NASA Advanced Propulsion Systems contract (5-year term)
- Completed acquisition of Orbital Microsystems Inc. for USD 240 million in Q3 2025
- Restatement of Q2 2024 revenue by USD 14.2 million (immaterial; disclosed pursuant
  to SEC Rule 10b-5 compliance obligations)
- Board approved a USD 300 million share buyback programme

PART II — RISK FACTOR DISCLOSURES
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

The following material risk factors are disclosed pursuant to Item 1A requirements:

RISK 1 — Government Contract Dependency
Approximately 61% of revenue is derived from U.S. government contracts (NASA, DoD, DARPA).
Any reduction in federal aerospace or defence spending, continuing resolution scenarios,
or contract termination for convenience could materially impact the Company's financial
results. The Company is subject to government audits, cost-accounting standards, and
mandatory cost-disclosure requirements.

RISK 2 — Export Control and ITAR Compliance
The Company's products and technology are subject to the International Traffic in Arms
Regulations (ITAR) and Export Administration Regulations (EAR). Failure to comply with
export control laws could result in fines, debarment from government contracting, and
reputational damage. The Company maintains an export compliance programme but cannot
guarantee continued compliance.

RISK 3 — Supply Chain Concentration
Approximately 34% of critical components are sourced from three primary suppliers.
Disruption to these suppliers due to geopolitical events, natural disasters, or financial
distress could delay production and increase costs. The Company is investing in supply
chain diversification, but near-term concentration risk remains elevated.

RISK 4 — Cybersecurity and Data Security
The Company's operations involve classified government data and proprietary aerospace IP.
A cybersecurity breach could compromise national security information, trigger regulatory
penalties, and damage customer relationships. The Company invested USD 28.5 million in
cybersecurity enhancements in fiscal 2025, including a new zero-trust architecture.

RISK 5 — Interest Rate and Debt Refinancing Risk
The Company carries USD 1.1 billion in long-term debt. Rising interest rates could
increase refinancing costs for the USD 400 million tranche maturing in November 2026.
A 100-basis-point increase in rates would increase annual interest expense by approximately
USD 11 million based on current floating-rate exposure.
