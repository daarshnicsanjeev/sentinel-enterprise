SECTION 1 — MATERIAL DISCLOSURE STATEMENT

This section constitutes the material disclosure statement required under SEC reporting rules.

On May 12, 2026, Meridian Financial Group, Inc. (“Company”) entered into an Agreement and Plan of Merger (“Merger Agreement”) with Eastbrook Capital Holdings Corp. (“Eastbrook”) and Eagle Merger Sub Inc., a wholly-owned subsidiary of the Company.

Pursuant to the Merger Agreement, Merger Sub will merge with and into Eastbrook, with Eastbrook surviving as a wholly-owned subsidiary of the Company. Total consideration: approximately USD 4.2 billion (USD 2.8 billion cash + USD 1.4 billion in newly issued shares). The Merger is subject to Eastbrook shareholder approval, Federal Reserve and OCC regulatory approvals, and other customary closing conditions. Expected close: Q4 2026.

In connection with the Merger Agreement, the Company has terminated its previously announced strategic review process initiated in January 2026, including all non-binding letters of intent with other potential counterparties. No termination fees are payable.

Material disclosure: The aggregate consideration payable represents a 31% premium to Eastbrook's 30-day volume-weighted average share price as of May 9, 2026. The transaction is expected to be accretive to earnings per share within 18 months of close, based on projected synergies of USD 180 million annually.

SECTION 2 — RISK FACTOR DISCLOSURES

The following material risk factors are disclosed pursuant to SEC reporting obligations:

Risk 1: Merger Integration Risk

The Company’s ability to realise the anticipated benefits of the Merger is subject to significant uncertainty. Integration of Eastbrook’s operations, technology systems, and workforce may take longer and cost more than anticipated. Failure to achieve projected synergies of USD 180 million annually could materially impact the combined entity’s financial performance and share price.

Risk 2: Regulatory Approval Risk

Completion of the Merger is contingent on regulatory approvals. Authorities may impose conditions including divestitures of business lines or geographic markets. There is no assurance approvals will be obtained on a timely basis or at all, which could delay or prevent the transaction.

Risk 3: Interest Rate Risk

Net interest income and the fair value of the investment portfolio are sensitive to rate changes. A sustained 100 basis point increase is estimated to reduce net interest income by USD 87 million over the next 12 months, based on the balance sheet composition as of March 31, 2026.

Risk 4: Credit Concentration Risk

Approximately 28% of the commercial real estate loan portfolio (USD 6.4 billion as of March 31, 2026) is concentrated in the New York metropolitan area. Deterioration in local market conditions could result in credit losses exceeding current reserves.

Risk 5: Cybersecurity Risk

The Company experienced a cybersecurity incident in February 2026 involving unauthorised access to certain internal systems. Contained within 48 hours; no customer data was compromised. Remediation cost: USD 12.3 million. Future incidents cannot be guaranteed to be similarly contained.
