1. MATERIAL DISCLOSURE

1.1 Nature and Scope of Processing
CloudStream Analytics Limited processes personal data of approximately 2.3 million EU/EEA data subjects across 27 member states in connection with its cloud-based business intelligence platform ("Platform"). The processing involves: behavioural analytics data, device identifiers, IP addresses, usage patterns, and where applicable, professional profile information for B2B customers.

1.2 High-Risk Processing Activities
This DPIA is submitted because the proposed processing activities involve: (a) systematic and extensive evaluation of personal aspects using automated processing, including profiling; (b) processing on a large scale of special categories of data as referred to in Article 9(1) GDPR; (c) systematic monitoring of a publicly accessible area on a large scale.

1.3 Legal Basis for Processing
The Company relies on the following legal bases under Article 6 GDPR: (a) Article 6(1)(b) — performance of a contract; (b) Article 6(1)(c) — compliance with legal obligation; (c) Article 6(1)(f) — legitimate interests, having conducted and documented a Legitimate Interests Assessment (LIA) dated January 10, 2026.

1.4 International Data Transfers
Personal data is transferred to the following third countries: United States (under Standard Contractual Clauses per Commission Decision 2021/914 and supplementary measures including encryption at rest and in transit, access controls, and regular audits); United Kingdom (adequacy decision in place per Article 45 GDPR).

2. RISK FACTOR DISCLOSURES

2.1 Identified Risks and Mitigations

RISK 1 — Unauthorised Access: Risk Level: HIGH
Potential for data breach through cyberattack or insider threat.
Mitigation: AES-256 encryption at rest; TLS 1.3 in transit; MFA enforced for all employees; quarterly penetration testing; SOC 2 Type II certification maintained.
Residual Risk after Mitigation: MEDIUM

RISK 2 — Algorithmic Bias in Profiling: Risk Level: MEDIUM
Automated profiling decisions may result in discriminatory outcomes.
Mitigation: Regular algorithmic audits conducted by independent third party; human review process for significant decisions; opt-out mechanism for profiling.
Residual Risk after Mitigation: LOW

RISK 3 — Data Subject Rights Fulfilment: Risk Level: MEDIUM
Volume of DSARs may exceed operational capacity.
Mitigation: Automated DSAR portal deployed; dedicated Privacy Operations team of 8 FTEs; SLA of 25 days for response (5 days inside statutory limit).
Residual Risk after Mitigation: LOW

RISK 4 — Processor Non-Compliance: Risk Level: MEDIUM
Sub-processors may not maintain adequate data protection standards.
Mitigation: Data Processing Agreements in place with all 23 sub-processors; annual audits of critical sub-processors; sub-processor register publicly available.
Residual Risk after Mitigation: LOW
