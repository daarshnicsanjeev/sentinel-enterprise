EMPLOYMENT AGREEMENT

This Employment Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of 19 May 2026 between
Helix Biotech Solutions Inc., a Delaware corporation ("Company") and
Dr. Marcus A. Osei ("Employee").

1. POSITION AND DUTIES
The Company hereby employs the Employee as Senior Principal Scientist, Genomics
Division. The Employee shall report to the Vice President of Research and Development
and shall perform such duties as are customarily associated with such position and
as may be reasonably assigned from time to time by the Company.

2. COMMENCEMENT AND LOCATION
Employment shall commence on 1 June 2026. The Employee's primary place of work
shall be the Company's research facility located at 2200 Discovery Drive,
San Diego, California 92121.

3. COMPENSATION AND BENEFITS CLAUSE
3.1 Base Salary. The Company shall pay the Employee a base salary of USD 185,000
per annum, payable bi-weekly in equal instalments, less applicable withholdings.

3.2 Annual Bonus. The Employee shall be eligible for an annual performance bonus
of up to thirty percent (30%) of base salary, based on individual and company
performance targets established annually by the Board of Directors.

3.3 Equity. The Employee shall be granted options to purchase 12,000 shares of
the Company's common stock, vesting over four (4) years with a one-year cliff,
pursuant to the Company's 2024 Equity Incentive Plan.

3.4 Benefits. The Employee shall be entitled to participate in all benefit plans
and programs generally made available to employees at the same level, including
health, dental, vision, life insurance, and a 401(k) plan with a Company match
of up to four percent (4%) of eligible compensation.

3.5 Expenses. The Company shall reimburse the Employee for reasonable and necessary
business expenses incurred in the performance of duties, subject to the Company's
expense reimbursement policy in effect from time to time.

4. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY ASSIGNMENT CLAUSE
4.1 Assignment of Inventions. The Employee hereby irrevocably assigns, transfers
and conveys to the Company, and agrees to assign to the Company, all right, title
and interest in and to any and all inventions, discoveries, developments,
improvements, innovations, software, works of authorship, trade secrets, know-how
and all other intellectual property (collectively, "Inventions") that the Employee
conceives, develops, reduces to practice or contributes to during the period of
employment that: (a) relate to the Company's actual or reasonably anticipated
business, research or development; or (b) result from or are suggested by any
work performed by the Employee for the Company; or (c) are made using the Company's
facilities, equipment, materials, personnel or Confidential Information.

4.2 Work for Hire. To the extent any Invention constitutes a "work made for hire"
under applicable copyright law, such Invention shall be the exclusive property of
the Company from the moment of its creation.

4.3 Prior Inventions. Schedule A sets out a complete list of inventions or
improvements that the Employee has, alone or jointly, conceived or reduced to
practice prior to the commencement of employment that the Employee wishes to
exclude from this assignment. If Schedule A is blank, the Employee represents
that no such prior inventions exist.

4.4 Cooperation. The Employee shall, both during and after employment, at the
Company's expense, execute all documents and take all actions reasonably necessary
to perfect, protect and enforce the Company's rights in any assigned Invention.

5. TERMINATION AND SEVERANCE CLAUSE
5.1 At-Will. Either party may terminate this Agreement at any time, with or without
cause, subject to the notice and severance provisions below.

5.2 Termination by Company Without Cause. If the Company terminates the Employee's
employment without Cause (as defined below), the Company shall pay the Employee:
(a) all accrued but unpaid base salary and vacation through the termination date;
(b) a severance payment equal to three (3) months of base salary, payable in a
lump sum within thirty (30) days of termination; and (c) continuation of health
benefits for three (3) months, or a cash equivalent if continuation is not
practicable. Such payments are conditioned on the Employee executing a general
release of claims in the Company's standard form within twenty-one (21) days of
receiving it.

5.3 Termination by Company for Cause. The Company may terminate employment
immediately for Cause. "Cause" means: (i) material breach of this Agreement not
cured within fifteen (15) days of written notice; (ii) conviction of, or guilty
plea to, any felony; (iii) gross negligence or wilful misconduct materially
harmful to the Company; or (iv) material violation of Company policy.

5.4 Resignation. The Employee shall provide thirty (30) days' written notice of
resignation. The Company may elect to waive all or part of the notice period and
pay in lieu of notice.

6. NON-COMPETE AND CONFIDENTIALITY CLAUSE
6.1 Confidentiality. During employment and for a period of five (5) years
thereafter, the Employee shall not use or disclose any Confidential Information
of the Company except as required for the performance of duties or as authorised
in writing by the Company. "Confidential Information" includes all non-public
technical, commercial and financial information relating to the Company's business,
including research data, clinical trial results, formulae, customer lists and
pricing information.

6.2 Non-Solicitation of Employees. During employment and for a period of two (2)
years after termination, the Employee shall not directly or indirectly solicit,
induce or recruit any employee of the Company to leave their employment.

6.3 Non-Solicitation of Customers. During employment and for a period of one (1)
year after termination, the Employee shall not solicit any customer or prospective
customer of the Company with whom the Employee had material contact in the twelve
(12) months preceding termination.

6.4 Non-Compete. During employment and for a period of one (1) year after
termination, the Employee shall not, within the United States, engage in or provide
services to any entity that is in direct competition with the Company's genomics
diagnostics business. The Company acknowledges that this restriction shall be
enforceable only to the extent permitted by applicable law.

7. GOVERNING LAW
This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of
the State of California. Any dispute shall be resolved exclusively in the state or
federal courts located in San Diego County, California.

8. ENTIRE AGREEMENT
This Agreement, together with any equity grant documents and Schedule A, constitutes
the entire agreement between the parties concerning the subject matter hereof and
supersedes all prior agreements, representations and understandings. Amendments
must be in writing signed by both parties.

IN WITNESS WHEREOF, the parties have executed this Agreement as of the date first
written above.
