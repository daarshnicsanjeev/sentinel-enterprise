SUMMER INTERNSHIP AGREEMENT

This Internship Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of May 1, 2026, between:

BRIGHTPATH CONSULTING GROUP, INC., a California corporation with offices at 350 California
Street, Suite 1200, San Francisco, CA 94104 ("Company"), and

ALEXANDER T. NGUYEN, an individual residing in Berkeley, California ("Intern").

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. INTERNSHIP POSITION AND DURATION
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

The Company hereby engages the Intern as a Summer Business Analyst Intern, commencing on
June 1, 2026, and concluding on August 31, 2026 (the "Internship Period"). The internship
is a temporary, educational engagement and does not create an employment relationship or
guarantee future employment.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
2. COMPENSATION AND BENEFITS CLAUSE
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

2.1  Stipend: The Intern shall receive a monthly stipend of USD 3,500, payable on the
last business day of each calendar month during the Internship Period.

2.2  Benefits: The Intern shall be eligible for the following during the Internship Period:
     (a) Access to the Company's employee assistance program (EAP);
     (b) Subsidized cafeteria meals at the San Francisco office;
     (c) A one-time technology allowance of USD 500 for software tools required for assigned
         projects, subject to prior written approval from the internship manager;
     (d) Participation in the Company's summer speaker series and professional development
         workshops at no cost.

2.3  Expenses: Pre-approved business travel expenses will be reimbursed in accordance with
the Company's expense reimbursement policy. The Intern must submit expense reports within
fifteen (15) days of incurring any reimbursable expense.

2.4  Taxes: The Intern is responsible for payment of all applicable taxes on the stipend.
The Company will issue an IRS Form 1099-NEC for any stipend amounts paid during the tax year.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
3. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY ASSIGNMENT CLAUSE
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

3.1  Work Product: Any and all work product, inventions, discoveries, developments,
improvements, processes, software, source code, documentation, analyses, reports, or other
materials created, conceived, or developed by the Intern, solely or jointly with others, in
connection with or arising from the Intern's activities during the Internship Period
(collectively, "Work Product") shall be the sole and exclusive property of the Company.

3.2  Assignment: The Intern hereby irrevocably assigns to the Company all right, title, and
interest in and to all Work Product, including all patents, copyrights, trade secrets, and
other intellectual property rights therein, throughout the world, in perpetuity.

3.3  Moral Rights Waiver: To the maximum extent permitted by applicable law, the Intern
hereby waives any and all moral rights or rights of attribution in connection with the
Work Product.

3.4  Further Assurances: The Intern agrees to execute any additional documents and take
any further actions as may be reasonably requested by the Company to evidence or perfect
the intellectual property assignment set forth herein.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
4. NON-COMPETE AND CONFIDENTIALITY CLAUSE
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

4.1  Confidentiality: During and after the Internship Period, the Intern shall maintain
in strict confidence all Confidential Information of the Company, its clients, and its
business partners. "Confidential Information" includes: client identities, project details,
proprietary methodologies, financial information, personnel data, business strategies,
and any information designated as confidential.

4.2  Non-Disclosure: The Intern shall not disclose, publish, or disseminate any Confidential
Information to any third party without the prior written consent of the Company.

4.3  Non-Solicitation: For a period of twelve (12) months following the end of the
Internship Period, the Intern shall not solicit, recruit, or induce any employee of the
Company to leave their employment.

4.4  Non-Competition: For a period of six (6) months following the end of the Internship
Period within a fifty (50) mile radius of San Francisco, CA, the Intern shall not accept
employment with or provide consulting services to any direct competitor of the Company in
the management consulting sector.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
5. GENERAL PROVISIONS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

5.1  Independent Contractor: The Intern shall perform services as an independent contractor.
Nothing in this Agreement shall create an employer-employee relationship.

5.2  Governing Law: This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of California.

5.3  Entire Agreement: This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties
with respect to the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior negotiations.

[NOTE: This internship agreement does not include a formal termination and severance clause
specifying grounds for early termination, notice periods, or severance entitlements. This
omission renders the agreement non-compliant with standard employment contract requirements.]
